# 10 questions for your AI vendor

*One-page reference · auxfirst · v0.1*

Take this into your next review. A vendor that can't answer Q1, Q6, or Q8 has a trust problem, not a tool problem.

---

1. **When the agent is wrong, does it say so, or pretend it isn't?**
2. **If my team tells it a preference today, will it remember next week?**
3. **What happens when the agent isn't sure — does it ask, guess, or refuse?**
4. **Who decided what the agent is and is not allowed to do without asking?**
5. **Where can our team see and edit what the agent remembers about us?**
6. **After a model or vendor update, how do you check that quality didn't drop?**
7. **What are the three most common complaints from users, and which of these named failures map to them?**
   - *memory amnesia, confident nonsense, preference ignored, overreach in ambiguity, metric over user, silent degradation*
8. **If the vendor's best answer is bad for us, will the agent surface that — or hide it?**
9. **What's the handoff to a human? How fast? Under what condition?**
10. **Can you show me one case where the agent was wrong, the fix, and the date it shipped?**

---

## Six failures in plain English

| What you see | What it's called |
|---|---|
| Agent forgets something your team told it. | memory amnesia |
| Agent confidently gives a wrong answer. | confident nonsense |
| Agent ignores an explicitly-set preference. | preference ignored |
| Agent acts on an ambiguous instruction. | overreach in ambiguity |
| Agent recommends the vendor-friendly option, not the user-friendly one. | metric over user |
| Agent quality drops after an update — silently. | silent degradation |

# The five principles

1. **GitHub is receipts.** Every claim ships with a schema, a CLI, or a reproducible scenario. Prose alone does not ship.
2. **Three repo kinds — Definition, Executable, Reference.** Every repo is exactly one. If a repo is two things, split it.
3. **Name before you tool.** The category wins when the language wins. ID first, implementation second.
4. **One taxonomy, two sides.** The internal (AUX Studio) and public (GitHub) layers share IDs. Rename once, propagate everywhere.
5. **Depth over breadth.** Five repos, two patterns, one canonical teardown. A sixth pattern only ships after the first two are complete, adopted, and stress-tested.
